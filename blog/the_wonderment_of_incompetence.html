The Wonderment of Incompetence

November 07, 2012

It isn't often that someone screws up this royally, but in a stroke of superior incompetence, I was able to make a backup of this blog's database, then make a backup of this site's content (in preparation for a server move) only to then delete both backups somehow, as well as cancelling the hosting for the site that they were on.

I've often treated this blog as second-class, so it's not really any surprise (to me) that I was able to screw it up, but what is surprising is that I had specifically planned against it, and perhaps just to having so much going on, was able to unravel said plans pretty fantastically.

Regardless, the point isn't to moan and whine about that as much as it is to explain where all the content went. I've gotten emails asking about articles, and in particular, the "Extremely Gentle Introduction" series, and I no longer have them. I'll likely get around to rewriting them based on the excerpts I've got, but if anybody is aware of full content caches of either the site, or those articles in particular, please point them my way as I would LOVE to get them back up and running.

I did find a backup of the "Extremely Gentle Introduction to Meteor", and have reposted it, but that's hardly the entire content of the site.

In completely related news, the blog is now one of those new fancy static blogs (using a modified version of Chisel for the moment), and content is versioned with Git, and backed up to Github. Apologies for the massive inconvenience, and I'll try harder to at least preserve the content here in the future.
