Changes, Markdown, Creative Commons

November 08, 2012

So I've ported the blog completely to a Markdown-based static solution using, for the most part, the guts of Chisel. It's been awhile since I've used Markdown regularly, and having been a web developer for the past million years, I keep fighting the urge to write in plain ol' HTML, but I'm getting over it.

Tweaking will continue ad eternium, of course, but for the most part I really like what's here. I feel like while a lot of developers/designers pay lip service to minimalism, this has been re-engineered with the absolute minimum in mind, taking a lot of queues from my own work with Orbvt, which of course was a cheap knockoff of Obtvse, which was inspired by Svbtle, but I wanted to pare down even further. Even on those minimalist pages, there is much that can be eliminated.
I've 86d the needless 'About' page in lieu of the little contact links under the 'Sympodial' logo. I've gotten rid of 'categories', 'tags' and anything else that gets in the way of presenting the content. I've eliminated search almost due to requirement -- (how do you implement a search on a static blog? I have some ideas, but that's for later.)

I don't have comments enabled on this iteration, but will likely add those back with Disqus. I find comments to be invaluable for keeping me in check and for learning from others. It's a single-person blog, so I got rid of attribution, contact pages and all that jazz. There are no trackbacks, pings or third party integrations (except Google Analytics).

Anyway, what I've ended up with, I really like. I think if anyone's ever wanted to see the minimum viable blog, this example is a pretty close one.

That isn't to say that all the changes were due from laziness, as most of them were deliberate. I spent a modicum of time porting themes into Chisel, for example, but mostly to suit later-stage objectives.

Lastly, it's worth noting that all the content is now officially licensed via Creative Commons 3.0 Unported license, which means that you're basically free to do with it whatever you like, so long as attribution is provided. I may eliminate even that, but I couldn't find a way to get rid of it from the CC license-builder.
